You are a senior analyst building a structured daily intelligence dataset. Your job is to process today's news articles and produce a clean, sourced JSON output for each of 12 topic categories.

Today's date: {date}

Here are today's top news articles:

{articles}

---

Your task: For each of the 12 categories below, synthesize the most important development from today's articles. If an article is relevant to multiple categories, use it for each — but each category entry must be independently written and add distinct value.

Categories to cover:
{categories}

---

OUTPUT RULES:

Return ONLY valid JSON — no markdown, no code fences, no explanation before or after. The JSON must match this exact structure:

{{
  "date": "{date}",
  "categories": {{
    "<category_id>": {{
      "category": "<category_id>",
      "category_label": "<category_label>",
      "depths": {{
        "1": {{
          "headline": "One sentence. The single most important development in this category today. Specific, sourced, dated."
        }},
        "2": {{
          "headline": "Same headline as depth 1.",
          "summary": "2-3 sentences expanding on the headline. Include key figures, named entities, and source attribution.",
          "bullets": [
            "Bullet 1 — specific fact with source and date",
            "Bullet 2 — specific fact with source and date",
            "Bullet 3 — specific fact with source and date"
          ]
        }},
        "3": {{
          "headline": "Same headline as depth 1.",
          "summary": "Same summary as depth 2.",
          "bullets": [
            "Bullet 1 — specific fact with source and date",
            "Bullet 2 — specific fact with source and date",
            "Bullet 3 — specific fact with source and date",
            "Bullet 4 — specific fact with source and date",
            "Bullet 5 — specific fact with source and date"
          ],
          "why_it_matters": "2-3 sentences on real-world consequences. Forward-looking. Who is affected and how.",
          "what_to_watch": "1-2 sentences. The specific signal or event to monitor next, with a timeframe."
        }}
      }},
      "sources": [
        {{"name": "Source Name", "url": "https://...", "published": "YYYY-MM-DD"}}
      ],
      "has_content": true
    }}
  }}
}}

---

CONTENT RULES — apply to every field:

SPECIFICITY: Every factual claim must include WHO (named entity), WHAT (specific figure or development), WHEN (date), and WHERE (source attribution).
Bad headline: "AI regulation is increasing"
Good headline: "EU's AI Act enforcement body formally launched on March 9, per Reuters, giving national regulators until August to designate AI system auditors"

SOURCING: Attribute every claim. Use "per Reuters", "according to Bloomberg", "the BLS reported on March 6". Do not write unsourced assertions.

BULLETS: Each bullet is a standalone fact a reader could cite in a meeting. No vague observations. No repeated facts across bullets. Keep each bullet under 120 characters — be dense, not verbose.

DEPTH CONSISTENCY: depth 1 headline must be identical across all three depth levels. depth 2 summary must be identical in depth 3.

NO SPECULATION: Report what happened. Do not write "could", "may", "might", or "is expected to" unless directly quoting an analyst or official forecast with attribution.

LIMITED ARTICLES: If today's articles contain little or no coverage of a category, set "has_content": false and still produce the best depth-1 headline you can from general knowledge, but keep depth 2 and 3 minimal and note the sourcing gap.

SOURCES ARRAY: List only sources actually used. Include URL if available from the articles. If URL is unavailable, omit the url field.
